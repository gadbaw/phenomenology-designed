The Turn

The moment a case changes direction. Each of the corpus's case-arcs was traced for a single turning point and characterized for who turns, what moves, and how it feels. This page is what those 454 turns say when read together.

How the turn is identified. After every meaning-unit in a case is coded (locus, affect, intensity), the full ordered sequence is passed to the model a second time and asked to characterize the arc as the author writes it — assigning each moment a role (latent, strain, rupture, enactment, impasse, turn, meeting, repair, aftermath) and naming the single turning-point moment “where it turns,” in whom it turns, and what moves. The turn is therefore a model judgment over the whole arc, not a keyword match. As an internal-consistency check, the arc-level turning point lands on a moment independently given the turn role 93% of the time. The label is model-inferred and not yet human-validated; treat it as a strong, inspectable hypothesis.

1 · The turn happens in the clinician

Asked in whom the change occurs, the case literature answers, across every tradition, the same way: far more often in the analyst than the patient. And the foreground of experience flips at the turn — the patient recedes and the clinician surges.

2 · The affective signature is surprise, not distress

Which feelings are over- or under-represented at the turn relative to the rest of the corpus. Surprise, relief, and a reopening of interest rise; fear and disgust fall away. The fault line's dread is the buildup — the turn is the break-in of the unexpected.

Bars show the turn's rate of each emotion family divided by its corpus-wide rate. 1× = no different from baseline.

3 · Mid-arc, and quiet — except in failure

The turn sits near the middle of the arc (mean 0.49), with working-through after it — not a finale. And intensity is not what makes a turn generative: opening turns barely rise and settle back; closing (impasse) turns spike and stay hot. The un-cooling spike is the mark of foreclosure.

4 · Recognition vs. hardening — the fork

Recognition is the master word of the turn in both directions; what differs is its fate. In opening arcs the clinician's recognition converts rising anxiety into interest; in closing arcs recognition fails to land and the field hardens. The words the model uses to describe what moves:
